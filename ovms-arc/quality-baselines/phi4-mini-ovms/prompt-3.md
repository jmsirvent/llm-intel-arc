Let's break down the problem step by step:

1. The first train leaves station A at 60 km/h.
2. Two hours later, the second train leaves station A at 90 km/h.

First, let's calculate how far the first train travels in those two hours before the second train departs:

Distance = Speed × Time
Distance = 60 km/h × 2 hours = 120 km

So, the first train is 120 km ahead of the second train when the second train starts.

Now, let's calculate the relative speed between the two trains. Since they are moving in the same direction, we subtract the speed of the first train from the speed of the second train:

Relative speed = Speed of second train - Speed of first train
Relative speed = 90 km/h - 60 km/h = 30 km/h

Now, we need to find out how long it takes for the second train to catch up to the first train. We can use the formula:

Time = Distance / Relative speed

We already know the distance the second train needs to cover to catch up to the first train is 120 km, and the relative speed is 30 km/h:

Time = 120 km / 30 km/h = 4 hours

So, the second train catches up to the first train 4 hours after it departs.
