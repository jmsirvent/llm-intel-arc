To determine how long after the second train departs it catches up to the first train, we can use the concept of relative speed and distance.

1. **Calculate the head start distance of the first train:**
   - The first train travels at 60 km/h.
   - It has a 2-hour head start before the second train starts.
   - Distance covered by the first train in 2 hours = Speed × Time = 60 km/h × 2 h = 120 km.

2. **Determine the relative speed between the two trains:**
   - The second train travels at 90 km/h.
   - The first train travels at 60 km/h.
   - Relative speed (second train - first train) = 90 km/h - 60 km/h = 30 km/h.

3. **Calculate the time taken for the second train to catch up:**
   - The second train needs to cover the 120 km gap at a relative speed of 30 km/h.
   - Time taken = Distance / Relative speed = 120 km / 30 km/h = 4 hours.

Therefore, the second train will catch up to the first train 4 hours after it departs.
