We are given:

- Train A leaves Station A at **60 km/h**.
- Two hours later, Train B leaves the same station at **90 km/h**.
- We are to find: **How long after Train B departs does it catch up to Train A?**

---

### Step 1: Determine how far Train A has traveled in the 2 hours before Train B departs

Train A is traveling at 60 km/h for 2 hours:

$$
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 60 \, \text{km/h} \times 2 \, \text{h} = 120 \, \text{km}
$$

So, when Train B starts, Train A is **120 km ahead**.

---

### Step 2: Let $ t $ be the time (in hours) after Train B departs that it catches up to Train A

In that time $ t $:

- Train B travels: $ 90t $ km
- Train A travels: $ 60t $ km

But Train A already had a 120 km head start, so the total distance Train A has traveled when Train B catches up is:

$$
120 + 60t
$$

At the moment Train B catches up, both trains have traveled the same distance:

$$
90t = 120 + 60t
$$

---

### Step 3: Solve the equation

$$
90t = 120 + 60t
$$

Subtract $ 60t $ from both sides:

$$
30t = 120
$$

Divide both sides by 30:

$$
t = \frac{120}{30} = 4
$$

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

**Train B catches up to Train A 4 hours after it departs.**

---

### Summary:

- Train A has a 2-hour head start → 120 km ahead.
- Train B travels faster (90 km/h vs. 60 km/h).
- After 4 hours, Train B has closed the 120 km gap.
- So, **4 hours after Train B departs**, it catches up to Train A.
