We are given:

- **Train 1** leaves **Station A** at **60 km/h**.
- **Train 2** leaves **Station A** **2 hours later**, going at **90 km/h**.
- We are to find **how long after Train 2 departs** it catches up to Train 1.

---

### Step 1: Understand the head start of Train 1

Train 1 has a **2-hour head start** before Train 2 starts moving.

In that time, Train 1 travels:

$$
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 60 \, \text{km/h} \times 2 \, \text{h} = 120 \, \text{km}
$$

So, when Train 2 starts, Train 1 is already **120 km ahead**.

---

### Step 2: Let time after Train 2 departs be $ t $ hours

Let $ t $ be the time (in hours) after Train 2 departs when it catches up to Train 1.

In that time:

- Train 2 travels: $ 90t $ km
- Train 1 travels: $ 60t $ km **in addition to the 120 km head start**

So, the total distance Train 1 has traveled when Train 2 catches up is:

$$
60t + 120 \, \text{km}
$$

---

### Step 3: Set distances equal (when they meet)

At the moment Train 2 catches up, both trains have traveled the same distance from Station A:

$$
90t = 60t + 120
$$

---

### Step 4: Solve the equation

$$
90t - 60t = 120 \\
30t = 120 \\
t = \frac{120}{30} = 4
$$

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

**The second train catches up to the first one 4 hours after it departs.**
