To solve this problem, we need to determine the time it takes for the second train to catch up with the first train after the second train departs. Let's break down the problem step by step.

1. **Determine the head start of the first train:**
   - The first train leaves station A at 60 km/h.
   - It has a 2-hour head start before the second train departs.
   - Distance covered by the first train in 2 hours = \( 60 \text{ km/h} \times 2 \text{ h} = 120 \text{ km} \).

2. **Set up the equations for the distances traveled by both trains:**
   - Let \( t \) be the time in hours after the second train departs when it catches the first train.
   - Distance traveled by the first train in \( t + 2 \) hours (since it started 2 hours earlier) = \( 60 \text{ km/h} \times (t + 2) \).
   - Distance traveled by the second train in \( t \) hours = \( 90 \text{ km/h} \times t \).

3. **Set the distances equal to each other since they meet at the same point:**
   \[
   60(t + 2) = 90t
   \]

4. **Solve for \( t \):**
   \[
   60t + 120 = 90t
   \]
   \[
   120 = 90t - 60t
   \]
   \[
   120 = 30t
   \]
   \[
   t = \frac{120}{30}
   \]
   \[
   t = 4 \text{ hours}
   \]

Therefore, the second train catches the first train 4 hours after it departs.
