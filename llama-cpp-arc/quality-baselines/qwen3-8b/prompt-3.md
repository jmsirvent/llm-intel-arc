We are given:

- **Train A** leaves **station A** at **60 km/h**.
- **Train B** leaves the **same station** **2 hours later** at **90 km/h**.
- We are to find: **How long after Train B departs does it catch up to Train A?**

---

### Step 1: Determine how far Train A has traveled before Train B starts

Train A has a **2-hour head start**.

$$
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 60 \, \text{km/h} \times 2 \, \text{h} = 120 \, \text{km}
$$

So, when Train B starts, Train A is **120 km ahead**.

---

### Step 2: Let $ t $ be the time (in hours) after Train B departs when it catches up to Train A

In that time:

- Train B travels: $ 90t $ km
- Train A travels: $ 60t $ km

But Train A already had a **120 km head start**, so the total distance Train A has traveled when Train B catches up is:

$$
60t + 120 \, \text{km}
$$

---

### Step 3: Set up the equation

When Train B catches up, both trains have traveled the **same distance**:

$$
90t = 60t + 120
$$

---

### Step 4: Solve the equation

$$
90t - 60t = 120 \\
30t = 120 \\
t = \frac{120}{30} = 4
$$

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

**Train B catches up to Train A 4 hours after it departs.**
