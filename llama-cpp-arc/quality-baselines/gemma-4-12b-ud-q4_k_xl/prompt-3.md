To find out how long it takes for the second train to catch the first, we can follow these steps:

### Step 1: Determine the head start of the first train
The first train travels at 60 km/h and has a two-hour head start before the second train departs.
$$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$$
$$\text{Distance} = 60 \text{ km/h} \times 2 \text{ hours} = 120 \text{ km}$$
When the second train starts, the first train is already **120 km** ahead.

### Step 2: Determine the relative speed
Since both trains are moving in the same direction, we need to find the "catch-up" speed (the difference between their speeds).
$$\text{Relative Speed} = \text{Speed of Train 2} - \text{Speed of Train 1}$$
$$\text{Relative Speed} = 90 \text{ km/h} - 60 \text{ km/h} = 30 \text{ km/h}$$
This means the second train closes the gap by **30 km every hour**.

### Step 3: Calculate the time to catch up
To find the time, we divide the initial distance gap by the relative speed.
$$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance Gap}}{\text{Relative Speed}}$$
$$\text{Time} = \frac{120 \text{ km}}{30 \text{ km/h}} = 4 \text{ hours}$$

***

**Final Answer:**
The second train will catch the first train **4 hours** after it departs.
