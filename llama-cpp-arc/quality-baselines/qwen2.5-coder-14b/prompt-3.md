To solve this problem, we need to determine the time it takes for the second train to catch up with the first train after the second train departs. Let's break down the problem step by step.

1. **Determine the head start of the first train:**
   - The first train leaves station A at 60 km/h.
   - Two hours before the second train departs, the first train has already been traveling.
   - Distance covered by the first train in 2 hours = Speed × Time = 60 km/h × 2 h = 120 km.

2. **Set up the equation for the distance traveled by each train:**
   - Let \( t \) be the time in hours after the second train departs.
   - Distance traveled by the first train in \( t + 2 \) hours (since it started 2 hours earlier) = 60 km/h × (t + 2) hours.
   - Distance traveled by the second train in \( t \) hours = 90 km/h × t hours.

3. **Set the distances equal to each other:**
   - When the second train catches the first train, they will have traveled the same distance from station A.
   - Therefore, we set the equations equal: 60(t + 2) = 90t.

4. **Solve for \( t \):**
   - Expand the left side of the equation: 60t + 120 = 90t.
   - Subtract 60t from both sides: 120 = 30t.
   - Divide both sides by 30: t = 4.

So, the second train catches the first train 4 hours after it departs.
