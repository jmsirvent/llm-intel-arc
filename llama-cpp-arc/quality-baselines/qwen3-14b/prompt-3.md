We are given:

- **Train 1** leaves **Station A** at **60 km/h**.
- **Train 2** leaves **Station A** **2 hours later** at **90 km/h**.
- We are to find **how long after Train 2 departs** it catches up to Train 1.

---

### Step 1: Define the time variables

Let’s define:

- Let **t** be the time (in hours) **after Train 2 departs** when it catches up to Train 1.

So, when Train 2 has been traveling for **t** hours, Train 1 has been traveling for **t + 2** hours (since it left 2 hours earlier).

---

### Step 2: Write the distance equations

- Distance = Speed × Time

So:

- Distance traveled by **Train 1** = $ 60(t + 2) $
- Distance traveled by **Train 2** = $ 90t $

At the moment Train 2 catches up to Train 1, they have traveled the **same distance**:

$$
60(t + 2) = 90t
$$

---

### Step 3: Solve the equation

$$
60(t + 2) = 90t
$$

Distribute:

$$
60t + 120 = 90t
$$

Subtract $60t$ from both sides:

$$
120 = 30t
$$

Divide both sides by 30:

$$
t = 4
$$

---

### ✅ Final Answer:

**The second train catches up to the first one 4 hours after it departs.**
