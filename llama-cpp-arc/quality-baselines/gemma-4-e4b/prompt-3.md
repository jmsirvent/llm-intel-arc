Here is the step-by-step solution to determine when the second train catches the first train.

### 1. Define Variables and Initial Conditions

*   **Train 1 Speed ($v_1$):** $60 \text{ km/h}$
*   **Train 2 Speed ($v_2$):** $90 \text{ km/h}$
*   **Time Head Start for Train 1 ($\Delta t$):** $2 \text{ hours}$

We are looking for the time ($t$) it takes for Train 2 to catch Train 1, measured from the moment Train 2 departs.

### 2. Calculate the Initial Distance Gap

Before Train 2 even starts, Train 1 has been traveling for 2 hours. We need to find the distance Train 1 has covered during this head start.

$$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$$
$$\text{Distance}_1 (\text{initial}) = v_1 \times \Delta t$$
$$\text{Distance}_1 (\text{initial}) = 60 \text{ km/h} \times 2 \text{ h}$$
$$\text{Distance}_1 (\text{initial}) = 120 \text{ km}$$

When Train 2 departs, Train 1 is $120 \text{ km}$ ahead.

### 3. Set Up the Catch-Up Equation

Let $t$ be the time (in hours) that passes *after* Train 2 departs until it catches Train 1.

*   **Distance covered by Train 1 after Train 2 starts:** $D_{1, \text{after}} = v_1 \times t = 60t$
*   **Total distance covered by Train 1 when caught:** $D_{1, \text{total}} = \text{Initial Gap} + D_{1, \text{after}} = 120 + 60t$
*   **Total distance covered by Train 2 when caught:** $D_{2, \text{total}} = v_2 \times t = 90t$

For Train 2 to catch Train 1, their total distances from Station A must be equal:
$$D_{2, \text{total}} = D_{1, \text{total}}$$
$$90t = 120 + 60t$$

### 4. Solve for Time ($t$)

Now, solve the equation for $t$:

1.  Subtract $60t$ from both sides:
    $$90t - 60t = 120$$
    $$30t = 120$$

2.  Divide by 30:
    $$t = \frac{120}{30}$$
    $$t = 4 \text{ hours}$$

---

### Answer

The second train catches the first train **4 hours** after the second train departs.

***

### Verification (Optional)

We can check the distance traveled by both trains after 4 hours:

*   **Train 2 Distance:** $90 \text{ km/h} \times 4 \text{ h} = 360 \text{ km}$
*   **Train 1 Total Time:** $2 \text{ hours (head start)} + 4 \text{ hours} = 6 \text{ hours}$
*   **Train 1 Distance:** $60 \text{ km/h} \times 6 \text{ h} = 360 \text{ km}$

Since both distances are $360 \text{ km}$, the calculation is correct.
