To determine how long after the second train departs it catches the first one, we can use the concept of relative speed and distance.

1. **Calculate the distance the first train travels in the two hours before the second train departs:**
   - Speed of the first train = 60 km/h
   - Time = 2 hours
   - Distance = Speed × Time = 60 km/h × 2 h = 120 km

2. **Determine the relative speed of the second train with respect to the first train:**
   - Speed of the second train = 90 km/h
   - Speed of the first train = 60 km/h
   - Relative speed = Speed of the second train - Speed of the first train = 90 km/h - 60 km/h = 30 km/h

3. **Calculate the time it takes for the second train to catch up to the first train:**
   - Distance the first train has traveled before the second train starts = 120 km
   - Relative speed = 30 km/h
   - Time to catch up = Distance / Relative speed = 120 km / 30 km/h = 4 hours

Therefore, the second train catches up to the first train 4 hours after the second train departs.
