Here is the step-by-step solution to the problem:

### 1. Define Variables and Initial Conditions

*   **Train 1 (T1):**
    *   Speed ($v_1$): $60 \text{ km/h}$
    *   Departure time: $t=0$
*   **Train 2 (T2):**
    *   Speed ($v_2$): $90 \text{ km/h}$
    *   Departure time: 2 hours after T1.

We want to find the time ($t$) it takes for Train 2 to catch Train 1, measured *from the moment Train 2 departs*.

### 2. Calculate the Head Start of Train 1

When Train 2 starts moving, Train 1 has already been traveling for 2 hours.

*   **Time Train 1 travels before Train 2 starts:** $t_{\text{head start}} = 2 \text{ hours}$
*   **Distance Train 1 has covered (Head Start Distance, $d_{\text{start}}$):**
    $$d_{\text{start}} = v_1 \times t_{\text{head start}}$$
    $$d_{\text{start}} = 60 \text{ km/h} \times 2 \text{ h} = 120 \text{ km}$$

At the moment Train 2 departs, Train 1 is $120 \text{ km}$ ahead.

### 3. Set Up the Catch-Up Equation

Let $t$ be the time (in hours) that Train 2 travels until it catches Train 1.

For Train 2 to catch Train 1, the total distance traveled by both trains must be equal at time $t$ (measured from when Train 2 starts).

*   **Distance traveled by Train 2 ($d_2$):**
    $$d_2 = v_2 \times t = 90t$$
*   **Distance traveled by Train 1 ($d_1$):** Train 1 has already traveled $120 \text{ km}$ plus the distance it travels during time $t$.
    $$d_1 = d_{\text{start}} + (v_1 \times t)$$
    $$d_1 = 120 + 60t$$

**The catch-up condition is:** $d_2 = d_1$

$$90t = 120 + 60t$$

### 4. Solve for Time ($t$)

Now, solve the equation for $t$:

1.  Subtract $60t$ from both sides:
    $$90t - 60t = 120$$
    $$30t = 120$$

2.  Divide by 30:
    $$t = \frac{120}{30}$$
    $$t = 4$$

### Answer

The second train catches the first train **4 hours** after the second train departs.
