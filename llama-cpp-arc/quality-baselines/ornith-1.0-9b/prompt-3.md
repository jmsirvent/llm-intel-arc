Here is the step-by-step reasoning to solve this problem:

### Step 1: Calculate the head start distance
First, we need to determine how far the first train has traveled before the second train even leaves.

*   **Speed of Train 1 ($v_1$):** $60 \text{ km/h}$
*   **Head start time ($t_{\text{head}}$):** $2 \text{ hours}$

$$ \text{Distance}_{\text{head}} = v_1 \times t_{\text{head}} $$
$$ \text{Distance}_{\text{head}} = 60 \text{ km/h} \times 2 \text{ h} = 120 \text{ km} $$

So, when the second train departs, the first train is already **120 km** ahead.

### Step 2: Determine the relative speed
Next, we calculate how much faster the second train is compared to the first train. This is the rate at which the second train closes the gap.

*   **Speed of Train 2 ($v_2$):** $90 \text{ km/h}$
*   **Relative speed ($v_{\text{rel}}$):**
$$ v_{\text{rel}} = v_2 - v_1 $$
$$ v_{\text{rel}} = 90 \text{ km/h} - 60 \text{ km/h} = 30 \text{ km/h} $$

The second train closes the gap at a rate of **30 km per hour**.

### Step 3: Calculate the time to catch up
Now we divide the initial gap distance by the relative speed to find out how long it takes to close that gap completely.

*   **Time to catch up ($t$):**
$$ t = \frac{\text{Distance}_{\text{head}}}{v_{\text{rel}}} $$
$$ t = \frac{120 \text{ km}}{30 \text{ km/h}} $$
$$ t = 4 \text{ hours} $$

### Alternative Method (Algebraic)
Let $t$ be the time in hours after the **second** train departs.
*   Distance traveled by Train 2: $90t$
*   Distance traveled by Train 1: $60(t + 2)$ (since it started 2 hours earlier)

Set the distances equal to find the catch-up point:
$$ 90t = 60(t + 2) $$
$$ 90t = 60t + 120 $$
$$ 90t - 60t = 120 $$
$$ 30t = 120 $$
$$ t = 4 $$

### Conclusion
The second train catches the first train **4 hours** after the second train departs.
